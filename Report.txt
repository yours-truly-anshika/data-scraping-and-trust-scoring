Multi-Source Scraper and Trust Scoring System
Technical Weighting Rationale

1) Objective
The trust score is designed to balance source reliability, evidence strength, freshness,
and compliance indicators while keeping the model interpretable and auditable.

2) Implemented Formula
TrustScore = (w1 * AC) + (w2 * CC) + (w3 * DA) + (w4 * R) + (w5 * MD)

Weights:
- AC (Author Credibility): 0.25
- CC (Citation Count): 0.20
- DA (Domain Authority): 0.20
- R (Recency): 0.20
- MD (Medical Disclaimer): 0.15

All weights are normalized in code if custom values are supplied.

3) Why AC Is Highest (0.25)
Author credibility is given the highest weight because attribution quality is a primary
signal in multi-source environments. Verified organizations have editorial controls,
review practices, and reputational constraints that materially reduce misinformation risk.

4) Why Recency and Authority Are Balanced (0.20 vs 0.20)
Recency (R = 0.20):
- Medical and technology information can become outdated quickly.
- Recency is modeled with exponential decay to prevent abrupt discontinuities.
- A recency cap (older than 5 years => max final score 0.7) guards against stale but
  otherwise strong-looking content.

Authority (DA = 0.20):
- Domain-level prior reliability is useful but should not dominate all other signals.
- DA remains equal to recency so old authoritative pages are not over-credited, and
  new low-authority pages are not over-rewarded.

This balance enforces practical fairness between source reputation and temporal relevance.

5) Why CC Is 0.20
Citation count is useful but non-linear in value; therefore log-normalization is used.
This prevents highly cited records from overwhelming the entire score while still
rewarding strong evidence density.

6) Why MD Is 0.15 + Explicit Penalties
Medical disclaimers are modeled as a direct compliance signal.
- Baseline contribution through MD weight = 0.15.
- Additional hard penalty for PubMed without disclaimer: 0.5 multiplier.
This two-layer treatment allows MD to influence normal ranking while still enforcing
strict abuse-prevention behavior in high-sensitivity contexts.

7) Range and Transparency Guarantees
- Output score is clamped to [0, 1].
- Every component (AC, CC, DA, R, MD) is exposed in code and independently testable.
- Penalty flags are surfaced in scoring outputs for auditability.

8) Conclusion
The selected weighting profile prioritizes identity and accountability (AC), then balances
source quality (DA), evidence (CC), and time relevance (R), while preserving a distinct
compliance channel (MD). This supports both integrity and explainability requirements for
production-grade trust scoring.
